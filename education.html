My School Journey

My college journey originally began with an interest in Computer Science while I was stationed at Fort Campbell, Kentucky. During that time, I completed a few core classes through Austin Peay State University (APSU) before eventually moving back to Georgia. After returning, I applied to the University of West Georgia and began studying Computer Science with a minor in Geographic Information Systems (GIS). That path gave me a strong starting point and helped confirm my interest in technology-related fields.

As my goals began to shift, I took a semester break to complete the ALETE Program through UWG and become a mandated police officer through Georgia POST. That experience was an important step in my professional development and influenced how I viewed both school and career planning. I am now pursuing a major in Computing with a focus in Cybersecurity, along with minors in GIS and Criminology. My education has changed over time, but each step has helped me build a path that better reflects my current goals and interests.

Future Goals

My future goals are centered on completing my degree while continuing to build experience in both technology and public service. I want my education to strengthen the skills I can apply in practical settings, especially in areas such as cybersecurity, analysis, and problem-solving. I am also interested in how my studies in GIS and Criminology connect with real-world work and decision-making. These academic areas give me a broader perspective and support the different career paths I have explored.

In the long term, I want to keep growing both professionally and personally by combining education with hands-on experience. My goal is not just to earn a degree, but to use what I learn in ways that are useful, meaningful, and connected to service. Because my path has already changed over time, I have learned that growth does not always happen in a straight line. What matters most is continuing to move forward with purpose and building a future that reflects both my interests and responsibilities.
